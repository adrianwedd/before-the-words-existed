# Research Task 3.2: Case vs. Molly Contrast

## Phase 3 | Track C Preparation | Priority: Medium

---

## Objective

Determine whether *Neuromancer* offers contrasting phenomenologies through Case and Molly—two different relationships to embodiment, stimulation, environment, and cognition.

The hypothesis:
- Case's cognition is optimised for disembodied information processing
- Molly's cognition is optimised for proprioceptive immediacy and somatic awareness
- These represent two different neurotypes, not one "normal" and one "aberrant"
- The novel encodes cognitive diversity, not just Case's pathology

**This task is necessary for Track C (academic paper) and optional for Track B (standalone essay).**

---

## Scope

**Primary text:** All scenes featuring Molly, with particular focus on:
- Shared scenes (Case and Molly together)
- Molly's solo action sequences
- Simstim sequences (Case experiencing through Molly's body)
- Their intimate/relational moments

**Analytical scope:** Systematic comparison across key phenomenological dimensions

---

## Why This Matters

If the essay claims *Neuromancer* encodes cognitive-environment mismatch, a reviewer will ask: "Does it encode *one* mismatch (Case's) or does it offer a comparative phenomenology?"

Molly is the test case. If she's simply "normal" (neurotypical baseline), Case becomes more pathological by contrast. If she represents a *different* phenomenology—equally specific, equally environmental-dependent—the novel's picture becomes richer and the argument stronger.

## Method

### Step 1: Molly Passage Inventory

Locate all significant Molly passages:
- Her introduction and physical description
- Solo action sequences
- Simstim sequences (Case riding her sensorium)
- Dialogue revealing her self-understanding
- Intimate moments with Case
- Moments of vulnerability or breakdown

### Step 2: Phenomenological Mapping

For each key passage, assess:
- **Embodiment:** How does she relate to her body?
- **Attention:** What does she notice? What does she track?
- **Time:** Any temporal markers?
- **Environment:** What affords her action? What blocks?
- **Affect:** What's her baseline mood/tone?
- **Identity:** How is selfhood constructed?

### Step 3: Simstim Analysis (Critical)

The simstim sequences are uniquely valuable: Case experiences *through Molly's body*.

Analyse:
- What does Case notice that Molly takes for granted?
- What overwhelms or surprises him about embodied experience?
- What does her sensorium feel like from inside?
- Is there a phenomenological contrast visible in these sequences?

### Step 4: Comparative Table

Build a systematic comparison across all dimensions.

### Step 5: Neurotype Hypothesis

Based on evidence, assess:
- Does Molly represent a contrasting cognitive style, or just a different character?
- If contrasting style, what characterises it?
- Is there any resonance with recognised neurodivergent phenotypes (without forcing diagnosis)?

## Key Questions to Answer

1. **Does Molly represent a contrasting phenomenology or just a different role?**
   - Contrast = different way of being in the world
   - Role = different function in the plot
   - Look for: phenomenological texture, not just action differences

2. **What is Molly's relationship to her body?**
   - Hypothesis: hyper-inhabited, augmented, weaponised
   - Test: does she dismiss the body? Does she over-identify with it?
   - Look for: how she talks about modifications, pain, sensation

3. **Is there evidence of a different stimulation need?**
   - Case needs cognitive stimulation (Matrix)
   - Does Molly need physical/sensory stimulation?
   - Look for: boredom patterns, what activates her, what flattens her

4. **What does Case learn from simstim that he didn't know?**
   - This is the phenomenological gold mine
   - His surprise reveals his own taken-for-granted assumptions
   - Her baseline reveals what she takes for granted

5. **Does Molly have her own "mismatch"?**
   - What environments don't work for her?
   - Is there a state analogous to Case's deprivation?
   - Look for: moments of dysfunction or unease

6. **Does the novel treat one phenomenology as normative?**
   - Or does it present both as valid, environmentally-dependent?
   - This affects the argument's strength significantly

---

## Counter-Reading to Engage

**The "Molly is just badass" reading:** Molly is a genre figure—the street samurai. Reading her as a contrasting neurotype over-interprets a stock character.

To address this:
- Acknowledge: she operates within genre conventions
- Distinguish: genre function vs. phenomenological texture
- Show: where the text gives her interiority that exceeds the type
- Test: do the simstim sequences offer more than action-movie spectacle?

---

## Possible Neurotype Resonances (Handle with Extreme Care)

If Molly represents a contrasting phenomenology, it might resonate with:

| Possibility | Markers to Look For | Risk |
|-------------|--------------------|----|
| Hypervigilance / trauma response | Constant threat-scanning, difficulty relaxing, somatic over-investment | Could pathologise her unfairly |
| Sensory-seeking phenotype | Need for physical intensity, boredom without action | Could be genre trope |
| Dissociation through action | Using physicality to avoid internal experience | Could be projection |

**Important:** Do not diagnose Molly. Note resonances only if the text strongly supports them. The goal is phenomenological description, not clinical classification.
