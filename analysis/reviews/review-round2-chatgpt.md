## 6) Historical Critique

This is where the essay bleeds out fastest, because your "naming gap" depends on being *right* about dates and dissemination.

* **Utah Criteria dating is wrong as written.** You cite "Wender et al., 1980s" and treat it as a research-enclave criterion set in that decade. But mainstream clinical summaries describe the Wender "Utah criteria" as an adult-focused set used and discussed in the 1990s, not the 1980s. ([PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12434367/))
* **Your own reference list bakes in the error** by listing "Wender… 1980s. Utah Criteria…" while later literature ties the Wender-Reimherr scale to "Wender, 1995." ([Ovid](https://www.ovid.com/journals/plast/fulltext/10.1037/a0032797))
* **DSM-III residual type framing:** You call ADD residual type a "thin diagnostic shadow" implying adult persistence should "evaporate." Historically, DSM-III did include residual type and it was explicitly part of evolving adult-ADHD nosology discussions in the 1980s–1990s; its later removal and renaming are documented, and the story is more complex than your rhetorical caricature. ([JAMA Network](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/373474))
* **Mike Oliver miscitation:** You cite "Oliver, 1983. 'The Individual and Social Models of Disability.' Paper presented at Thames Polytechnic." That specific "Individual and Social Models" paper is widely circulated as a **1990** workshop paper, while Oliver's **1983** contribution is typically associated with *Social Work With Disabled People* and coining/popularizing "social model" language. ([The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/society/2019/mar/19/mike-oliver-obituary))
* **Neurodiversity timeline needs sourcing, not vibes.** You assert "neurotypical… 1996… neurodiversity 1998… neurodivergent ~2000." Those dates are broadly defensible, but you don't cite the primary/secondary history you'd need to make them stick. ([National Autistic Society](https://www.autism.org.uk/advice-and-guidance/topics/identity/the-neurodiversity-movement))

Net: the historical scaffold currently contains **hard errors** and **soft overstatements**. If a reviewer catches two, they'll assume ten.
